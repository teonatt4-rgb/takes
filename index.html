Approach自述

I was trained to render a score faithfully. Jazz taught me to ask a different question: not how to play a piece, but how to make it mine.

Strings, the European canon, the discipline of notation — that is where I come from. Jazz arrived later, and the violin is a stranger in it; that distance is exactly what I want to explore. Across four years of listening, transcribing and deciding, I can no longer trace where each phrase began — which is precisely when a borrowed language starts to become a voice.

I think of improvising and arranging as a human generative system — one that now stands beside machine generation, in dialogue with it rather than replaced by it. That question runs through everything here, and becomes the explicit subject of my original work, Dream of Martian Consciousness. The classical canon is not the whole of music; jazz, and the music still being written now, leaves so much left to explore. That is the work I intend to keep doing.

我受的训练，是忠实地呈现一份乐谱；而爵士教会我问另一个问题——不是"如何演奏一首曲子"，而是"如何让它成为我的"。弦乐、欧洲经典、记谱的纪律，是我的来处；爵士来得更晚，小提琴在其中是个外来者，而这段距离正是我想探索的。四年的聆听、扒谱与抉择之后，我已记不清每个乐句的起点——而那恰恰是"借来的语言"开始成为"声音"的时刻。我把即兴与编配视为一种"人的生成系统"，它与今天的机器生成并置、对话，而非被取代。这个命题贯穿这里的一切，并在我的原创作品《梦的火星意识》中成为明确的主题。古典并非音乐的全部；爵士，以及此刻仍在被书写的音乐，还有那么多值得探索的地方。这就是我想一直做下去的事。

Classical was where I began; jazz is where I keep asking. This is the voice I'm still building — one record at a time. 古典是我的起点，爵士是我不断发问的地方。这是我仍在塑造的声音——一张唱片，一张唱片地。

— 梁珏童 Liang Juetong
